Best free AI tools

You do not need to spend anything to get real value from AI. These are the tools where the free version is genuinely good enough for most people — not crippled demos, but actual useful tools.

✅
Our honest approach to free tools

We only list tools where the free tier is genuinely useful — not ones where it exists just to frustrate you into paying. We note all limitations clearly.

1. ChatGPT — best free general AI tool

The free version of ChatGPT handles writing, research, summarising, answering questions, and generating ideas. There are daily usage limits, but for most people who are new to AI, the free tier is more than enough to start.

What the free tier includes: Full access to GPT-4o mini. Unlimited basic use. Some limits on advanced model access during peak times.
Free tier limitations: Daily usage limits on the most powerful model. No image generation. No memory of previous conversations.
Try ChatGPT free → AFFILIATE

2. Claude — best free tool for careful writing

Claude's free tier is strong. It handles professional emails, document summarisation, and research questions with noticeably more care and precision than many alternatives. Worth trying alongside ChatGPT.

What the free tier includes: Access to Claude Sonnet. Genuine usefulness for writing and research tasks.
Free tier limitations: Daily message limits. No file uploads on the free plan.
Try Claude free →

3. Google Gemini — best free Google integration

Gemini's free tier connects directly to Gmail, Google Docs, and Google Drive. If you use Google for work, the free version alone can meaningfully change how you handle emails and documents.

Try Gemini free →

4. Canva — best free image and design tool

Canva's free tier gives you access to thousands of templates, basic AI image generation, and the ability to create professional social media graphics, presentations, and documents with no design experience.

Try Canva free → AFFILIATE

5. Grammarly — best free writing assistant

Install it once as a browser extension and it works quietly in the background — catching grammar errors, improving sentences, and flagging unclear writing across every website and email you use.

Install Grammarly free → AFFILIATE

6. Perplexity — best free research tool

Unlike ChatGPT or Claude, Perplexity searches the web and cites its sources. The free version is excellent for research, fact-checking, and getting up-to-date information with references you can actually check.

Are free AI tools safe to use?

+

For general tasks, yes. Be careful about pasting sensitive personal data, customer information, or confidential business details into any AI tool. Read the privacy policy of any tool you use regularly.

When is it worth paying for an AI tool?

+

When you are hitting the free tier limits regularly and the time saved is clearly worth more than the subscription cost. For most small business owners, ChatGPT Plus at $20/month (~£16) pays for itself in the first week.

Do free AI tools sell my data?

+

Policies vary. Most major tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) allow you to opt out of having your conversations used for training. Check the privacy settings in each tool after signing up.
