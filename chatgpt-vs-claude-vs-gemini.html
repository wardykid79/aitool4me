ChatGPT — the most versatile option

ChatGPT is the most widely used AI tool in the world. It handles the widest range of tasks, has the largest community of users, and the best ecosystem of integrations with other apps.

✅ StrengthsMost versatile. Huge range of uses. Strong free tier. Best plugin and integration ecosystem.
⚠️ WeaknessesCan produce confident mistakes. Free version has usage limits. Needs clear prompting.

Best for: general use, research, drafting, customer communication, social content, business planning.

Claude — the most careful writer

Claude is built by Anthropic and is known for producing more measured, thoughtful, and professional writing. It is less likely to make things up, and tends to be more honest when it is uncertain about something.

✅ StrengthsExcellent writing quality. More careful and accurate. Very good at following nuanced instructions.
⚠️ WeaknessesFewer third-party integrations. Not yet in as many tools as ChatGPT.

Best for: professional emails, proposals, business writing, summarising documents, anything where tone matters.

Google Gemini — best for Google Workspace users

Gemini integrates directly with Gmail, Google Docs, Drive, and Calendar. If your business already runs on Google tools, Gemini can summarise your emails, draft documents, and find files — all without leaving the apps you already use.

✅ StrengthsDeep Google integration. No extra apps needed. Strong free tier. Works inside Gmail and Docs.
⚠️ WeaknessesLess useful if you do not use Google. Still catching up on some tasks.

Best for: anyone already using Google Workspace, Gmail summarisation, Docs drafting, Drive search.

Our verdict: which should you use?

If you are new to AI

Start with ChatGPT free. It handles the widest range of tasks and has the most help and tutorials available.

If writing quality matters

Use Claude for anything involving professional communication, proposals, or customer-facing writing.

If you use Google

Use Gemini — the integration with Gmail and Docs alone is worth it if you live in Google Workspace.

Can I use more than one AI tool?

+

Yes — and many people do. A common setup is ChatGPT for general tasks, Claude for important writing, and Canva for visuals. You are not locked in to one tool.

Are paid plans worth it?

+

For regular business use, probably yes. The free tiers have daily limits that you will hit quickly if you use AI consistently. ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro both unlock significantly more capability for roughly the cost of a lunch per month.

Which AI tool is most accurate?

+

All three make mistakes. Claude tends to be more cautious about claiming certainty. Perplexity is better if you need sourced, verifiable information. For any important decision, always verify AI output independently.
