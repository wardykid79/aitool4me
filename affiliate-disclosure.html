Affiliate Disclosure

Honest and transparent. Always.

Last updated: June 2026

Plain-English summary: Some links on AI Tool 4 Me are affiliate links. If you click one and sign up or buy something, we may earn a small commission — at no extra cost to you. We only recommend tools we have reviewed as relevant. Affiliate links are always labelled on the page.

What is an affiliate link?

When a website links to a product or service using a special tracking link, and someone clicks that link and makes a purchase or signs up, the website may earn a commission. This is called affiliate marketing.

The commission is paid by the company whose product you bought — not by you. The price you pay is exactly the same whether you use our link or go directly to the provider's website.

How we use affiliate links

Some tool comparison pages, review pages, and guide pages on AI Tool 4 Me contain affiliate links. When they do:

  • The link is labelled clearly with the text "Affiliate link" near the button
  • The button uses rel="nofollow sponsored" attributes as required by Google
  • We do not hide, disguise, or misrepresent affiliate links

Our principles

  • We only include tools that appear relevant to the page topic and the visitor's likely needs
  • Affiliate relationships do not change what we write about a tool — pros, cons, and limitations are always included
  • We do not accept payment to change or inflate a recommendation
  • We do not recommend tools we have not reviewed
  • Free alternatives are always shown where they exist
  • All tool pricing is shown with a "last checked" date — we verify every 90 days
  • We disclose affiliate links on every page where they appear

Which programmes do we use?

We work with affiliate programmes from AI tools, writing tools, automation tools, email marketing platforms, design tools, and hosting providers. A full list of programmes we have applied to or participate in can be found on our affiliate programmes page.

FTC and UK compliance

This disclosure is made in accordance with the FTC's guidelines on endorsements and testimonials, and the UK's CAP Code on advertising standards. We believe in transparency — not because we are required to, but because it is the right thing to do.
