What is AI?
A plain English explanation

Artificial intelligence is everywhere right now — but most explanations are written for engineers, not everyday people. This guide explains AI in plain English: what it actually is, what it can and cannot do, and how to start using it safely.

The basics

What is AI in simple terms?

AI — artificial intelligence — is software that can understand language, answer questions, write text, create images, and carry out tasks that previously needed a human to do them.

The most useful AI tools today work like this: you type what you need — a question, a request, a piece of writing — and the AI responds with something useful. It's a bit like having a very capable, very fast assistant who never sleeps and never gets annoyed.

The important thing to understand is that AI doesn't think the way humans do. It doesn't understand anything, really. It's extremely good at recognising patterns in text and producing useful-sounding responses — but it can be wrong, sometimes confidently. That's why a human always needs to stay in the loop.

⚠️
AI is powerful — but not always right.

Never trust AI alone for legal, medical, tax, or financial decisions. It makes confident mistakes. Always treat its output as a first draft that needs a human to review it.

What can AI actually do?

Here are the things AI is genuinely good at right now — not in theory, but in practice, today, for real people and small businesses:

✍️

Write and improve text

Emails, website copy, social posts, proposals — drafted or polished in seconds. You describe what you need; AI produces a first draft.

🧠

Answer questions

Ask anything in plain English and get a clear, detailed answer. Like a knowledgeable colleague available 24 hours a day.

📄

Summarise documents

Paste in a long contract, report, or article. AI will pull out the key points in plain language — in seconds.

🎨

Create images and visuals

Generate professional graphics, marketing images, and illustrations — no design experience needed.

⚡

Automate repetitive tasks

Connect your apps and let AI handle the tasks that eat your week — data entry, emails, notifications, scheduling.

🔍

Research and summarise

Ask AI to research a topic, compare options, or find information — it can do in minutes what might take you hours.

What AI cannot do — and where to be careful

Understanding the limits is just as important as knowing the capabilities. AI is not good at everything.

❌ Do not rely on AI alone for:

  • Legal advice or contract decisions
  • Medical diagnoses or health decisions
  • Tax returns or financial advice
  • Anything where being wrong has serious consequences
  • Current news or very recent events

✅ AI works well for:

  • First drafts of emails and documents
  • Explaining complex topics simply
  • Summarising long documents
  • Generating ideas and options
  • Automating repetitive communication tasks

Frequently asked questions

Is AI the same as robots?

+

No. Most AI today is software — programs you interact with by typing or speaking. Physical robots are a separate area. The AI tools on this site are all software you use on your phone or computer.

Do I need to be technical to use AI?

+

Not at all. Modern AI tools work by typing in plain English — just like sending a text message. If you can type, you can use AI.

Is AI going to take my job?

+

AI is changing many jobs, but for most people right now it's more useful as a tool that helps you do your job faster and better — not a replacement. The people most at risk are those who refuse to learn how to use it.

Is AI free to use?

+

Most leading AI tools have a genuinely useful free tier. ChatGPT, Claude, and Google Gemini all offer free versions. Paid plans unlock more usage and advanced features, but you can get real value without spending anything.

How do I know if AI output is correct?

+

You read it critically and check anything important. AI makes mistakes — sometimes very confidently. Always treat AI output as a first draft. For anything legally, medically, or financially significant, get a professional to review it.
